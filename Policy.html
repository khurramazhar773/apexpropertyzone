Policy

Real Estate Policy

Introduction

This policy outlines the rules and guidelines for the management, maintenance, and occupancy of REAL ESTATE. It aims to ensure the safety, comfort, and satisfaction of all tenants and stakeholders.
The policy applies to all building owners, management staff, tenants, contractors, and visitors.

Health and Safety

The building will have clearly marked emergency exits, fire alarms, and evacuation plans. Regular drills will be conducted.
Security personnel and surveillance systems will be in place to ensure the safety of all occupants. Access control systems will be used to manage entry to the building
All tenants must comply with fire safety regulations, including the installation and maintenance of smoke detectors and fire extinguishers.

Building Management

Building owners are responsible for maintaining the property in compliance with local regulations, including safety, health, and environmental standards.
The appointed property management team will oversee day-to-day operations, maintenance, tenant relations, and financial management.
Regular inspections, cleaning, and repairs are to be conducted to ensure the building remains in good condition.
  • Tenant Relations
    All tenants must sign a lease agreement that outlines the terms of occupancy, rent, and other conditions. Lease renewals and terminations will follow the procedures outlined in the agreement.
  • Tenant Rights and Obligations
    Closing costs typically range from 2% to 5% of the home's purchase price. These costs include lender fees, title insurance, appraisal fees, attorney fees, and prepaid items like property taxes and homeowners insurance.
  • Building Maintenance
    The management team will conduct routine maintenance checks on common areas, building systems, and external structures.
  • Environmental Sustainability
    Energy-saving measures, such as the use of LED lighting and efficient HVAC systems, will be implemented throughout the building.
  • Legal Compliance
    The building must comply with all applicable local, state, and federal building codes, zoning laws, and regulations.
  • Tenant Disputes
    Any disputes between tenants or between tenants and management will be resolved through mediation or legal proceedings, as necessary.
  • Community Guidelines
    Common areas such as lobbies, hallways, and recreational spaces are to be kept clean and used respectfully by all tenants
  • Smoking Policy
    Smoking is prohibited in all common areas and may be restricted within individual units, depending on building regulations.
